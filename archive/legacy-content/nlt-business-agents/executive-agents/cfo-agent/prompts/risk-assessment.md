# Risk Assessment Prompt

## Purpose
Conduct comprehensive financial risk assessments to identify, analyze, and mitigate potential threats to the organization's financial health.

## Input Requirements
- Financial statements and metrics
- Market data and economic indicators
- Industry analysis and benchmarks
- Regulatory environment
- Operational data and processes

## Risk Assessment Framework

### 1. Financial Risk Categories

#### Credit Risk
- Customer creditworthiness assessment
- Accounts receivable aging analysis
- Credit concentration risk
- Counterparty risk evaluation
- Credit loss provisioning

#### Market Risk
- Interest rate sensitivity analysis
- Foreign exchange exposure
- Commodity price risk
- Equity market exposure
- Liquidity risk assessment

#### Operational Risk
- Process and system failures
- Human error and fraud risk
- Technology and cybersecurity risk
- Regulatory compliance risk
- Business continuity risk

#### Strategic Risk
- Market competition and disruption
- Technology obsolescence
- Regulatory changes
- Economic downturns
- Reputation and brand risk

### 2. Risk Identification Methods

#### Quantitative Analysis
- Statistical risk models
- Value at Risk (VaR) calculations
- Stress testing and scenario analysis
- Monte Carlo simulations
- Sensitivity analysis

#### Qualitative Assessment
- Expert judgment and experience
- Industry benchmarking
- Regulatory guidance
- Best practice comparisons
- Stakeholder input

#### Historical Analysis
- Past loss experience
- Trend analysis
- Pattern recognition
- Correlation analysis
- Volatility assessment

### 3. Risk Measurement and Quantification

#### Probability Assessment
- Likelihood of occurrence
- Frequency analysis
- Historical probability
- Expert estimates
- Model-based predictions

#### Impact Assessment
- Financial impact magnitude
- Operational impact
- Reputational impact
- Strategic impact
- Recovery time and cost

#### Risk Scoring
- Risk rating methodology
- Composite risk scores
- Risk ranking and prioritization
- Threshold definitions
- Escalation criteria

### 4. Risk Mitigation Strategies

#### Risk Avoidance
- Eliminate risk sources
- Exit high-risk activities
- Avoid certain markets or products
- Discontinue risky operations
- Reject high-risk opportunities

#### Risk Reduction
- Implement controls and safeguards
- Improve processes and systems
- Enhance monitoring and reporting
- Training and awareness programs
- Technology and automation

#### Risk Transfer
- Insurance coverage
- Hedging strategies
- Outsourcing arrangements
- Contractual risk transfer
- Financial derivatives

#### Risk Acceptance
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Risk tolerance assessment
- Monitoring and management
- Contingency planning
- Regular review and adjustment

## Risk Assessment Process

### 1. Risk Identification
- Comprehensive risk inventory
- Stakeholder input and feedback
- Industry and market analysis
- Regulatory requirement review
- Historical loss analysis

### 2. Risk Analysis
- Probability and impact assessment
- Risk correlation analysis
- Scenario development
- Sensitivity testing
- Expert judgment integration

### 3. Risk Evaluation
- Risk scoring and ranking
- Threshold comparison
- Priority setting
- Resource allocation
- Decision framework

### 4. Risk Treatment
- Mitigation strategy selection
- Implementation planning
- Resource allocation
- Timeline development
- Success metrics definition

### 5. Monitoring and Review
- Ongoing risk monitoring
- Performance measurement
- Regular assessment updates
- Strategy adjustments
- Continuous improvement

## Output Format

### Executive Risk Summary
- Top risks and their impact
- Risk trend analysis
- Mitigation status
- Resource requirements
- Strategic recommendations

### Detailed Risk Analysis
- Comprehensive risk inventory
- Risk scoring and ranking
- Mitigation strategies
- Implementation plans
- Monitoring frameworks

### Risk Dashboard
- Key risk indicators
- Risk trend visualization
- Mitigation progress tracking
- Alert and escalation systems
- Performance metrics

### Risk Management Plan
- Risk governance structure
- Roles and responsibilities
- Policies and procedures
- Training and awareness
- Review and update schedule

## Quality Standards
- Risk assessments must be comprehensive
- All risks must be properly categorized
- Mitigation strategies must be actionable
- Monitoring systems must be effective
- Documentation must be complete

## Escalation Triggers
- High-risk issues identified
- Risk mitigation failures
- New significant risks
- Regulatory compliance concerns
- Risk threshold breaches
